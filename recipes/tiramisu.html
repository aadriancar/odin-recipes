Tiramisu Recipe

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert made with layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, a rich mascarpone filling, and homemade whipped cream. The dessert is traditionally flavored with Marsala wine, but variations may use rum or none at all, relying solely on strong coffee. Tiramisu, meaning "pick-me-up" in Italian, was invented in the 1970s by renowned Italian restaurateur Ado Campeol, who is often called the "father of tiramisu." It has since become a beloved treat worldwide.

To prepare tiramisu, the filling is made by cooking egg yolks, sugar, and milk until thickened, then chilling the mixture before adding mascarpone cheese. Heavy cream is whipped into stiff peaks to create a smooth layer of whipped cream. Ladyfingers are soaked in a coffee and rum mixture before being layered in a dish. The mascarpone mixture is spread over the soaked ladyfingers, followed by the whipped cream. The process is repeated, then the dessert is dusted with cocoa powder for a finishing touch.

Ingredients

  • Egg yolks
  • Sugar
  • Milk
  • Cream
  • Vanilla extract
  • Mascarpone cheese
  • Coffee
  • Rum
  • Ladyfingers
  • Cocoa powder

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Whisk egg yolks and sugar together in a medium saucepan until well blended.
  3. Whisk in milk and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until mixture comes to a boil.
  4. Boil gently for 1 minute, then remove from the heat and allow to cool slightly.
  5. Cover tightly and chill in the refrigerator for 1 hour.
  6. Beat cream and vanilla in a medium bowl with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form.
  7. Remove egg yolk mixture from the refrigerator; add mascarpone cheese and whisk until smooth.
  8. Combine coffee and rum in a small bowl. Split ladyfingers in half lengthwise and drizzle with the coffee mixture. Arrange 1/2 of the soaked ladyfingers in the bottom of a 7x11-inch dish.
  9. Spread 1/2 of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, then spread 1/2 of the whipped cream over top. Repeat layers once more.
  10. Sprinkle cocoa powder over top.
  11. Cover and refrigerate until set, 4 to 6 hours.
  12. Enjoy!
